About

MacDevs is a YouTube channel for Mac developers where misfits, rebels, troublemakers, and the ones who think differently share their knowledge about developing Mac apps. Videos are recorded by the speakers and information is provided so viewers can contact presenters with questions or comments. See the sections below if you would like to submit a video to the channel.

Submit your talk

If you would like to give a presentation or demo, please submit your talk via email. Include the items listed below in your submission. Keep in mind that presentations and demos should be less than 30 minutes. Accepted speakers will receive a follow-up email on how to submit their video.

  1. Presentation title
  2. Presenter's first and last name
  3. A brief description of your talk or demo
  4. Links to your GitHub, Twitter, personal website, or blog

Below are some ideas for presentations. Other topics are welcome as long as they are relevant to developing Mac applications.

  • Command line tools built with Swift, Rust, Python, C++, etc.
  • Server side programming with Swift
  • Machine learning on macOS
  • Using Swift and SwiftUI for Mac app development
  • Utilize C++, Python, and other languages in a native Mac app
  • GPU programming with Metal
  • Scientific computing with Swift and Accelerate
  • Distributing Mac apps outside the App Store
  • Creating Mac apps with Tkinter, wxPython, PyQt, etc. using Python
  • Develop and deploy Safari extensions

Recording your talk

Presenters should record their presentation or demo at fullscreen resolution. This will ensure the videos are encoded at the highest quality for YouTube. Try to keep the duration of the video to 30 minutes or less. Presenters will be given a Dropbox link for uploading their video. See the resources provided below for information about recording videos along with some recommended software and hardware options.

  • How to record computer screen and webcam using QuickTime
  • How to record Keynote and webcam using QuickTime and iMovie
